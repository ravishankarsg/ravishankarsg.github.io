About

Translating evidence into clinical practice

I am a Research Fellow at the Clinical Research and Innovation Office (CRIO), Tan Tock Seng Hospital, where I lead the methodological and analytical core of the Innovation and Research Implementation Support (IRIS) Programme. My work supports clinician investigators across the translational pipeline, from systematic evidence synthesis and health economic modelling to the design, deployment, and evaluation of clinical AI systems in real-world workflows.

My current research centres on language as a clinical signal. As Principal Investigator of an NMRC Healthy Longevity Catalyst Award, I am developing AI-powered speech analysis methods to track and predict cognitive impairment in stroke rehabilitation. In parallel, I build and evaluate privacy-preserving large language model pipelines for structured extraction from clinical documents, spanning oncology pathology reports, registry curation, dialysis records, and clinical trial pre-screening.

I came to healthcare research through engineering. Trained at IIT Madras and the National University of Singapore, I bring a systems perspective to clinical problems: rigorous measurement, careful economic evaluation, and a sustained focus on whether innovations actually survive contact with practice.

Research Focus

Four connected programmes of work

Clinical AI and Natural Language Processing

Design and evaluation of large language model pipelines for clinical information extraction, summarisation, and decision support, with an emphasis on locally deployed, privacy-preserving inference within hospital infrastructure. Active applications include oncology pathology extraction, registry curation, haemodialysis summarisation, and trial pre-screening.

Local LLM inferenceInformation extractionAI governanceObservability

Rehabilitation Research and Assistive Technology

Design, evaluation, and validation of rehabilitation technologies across stroke and spinal cord injury, including telerehabilitation robotics, robotic exoskeletons, transcutaneous spinal cord stimulation and neuromodulation, exergaming, and markerless gait analysis, from feasibility studies through pragmatic multi-site trials.

Implementation Science and Evidence Synthesis

Systematic reviews, qualitative studies, and framework-driven implementation research (CFIR, RE-AIM, NPT, TFA) examining how digital health and rehabilitation technologies are adopted, sustained, and scaled in real healthcare systems.

Selected Projects

Active and recent work

Principal Investigator NMRC HLCA · 2024–present

AI-powered speech analysis for post-stroke cognitive impairment

A mixed methods pilot study developing and validating speech-based AI markers to track and predict cognitive impairment during stroke rehabilitation, with the goal of enabling earlier, lower-burden cognitive monitoring in clinical care.

Funder: National Medical Research Council, Healthy Longevity Catalyst Award (HLCA24Mar-0007)
Lead, AI Pipelines Clinical NLP · Ongoing

Privacy-preserving LLM pipelines for clinical data extraction

A family of locally deployed large language model pipelines for structured extraction and summarisation across breast cancer pathology reports, oncology registry data, haemodialysis flowsheets, and clinical trial pre-screening, designed to run entirely within hospital infrastructure without external data transfer.

Setting: Tan Tock Seng Hospital, National Healthcare Group
Principal Investigator Synapxe GenAIus · 2024–present

AI-driven nursing shift handover assistant

Development and evaluation of a generative AI assistant for nursing shift handover, a winning entry in the national Synapxe GenAIus Challenge, including phenomenological research on nurses' lived experiences of AI-enabled handover innovation.

Funder: Synapxe, Singapore
Principal Investigator Caregiver Support · Ongoing

Digital interventions for caregiver wellbeing in kidney disease

Two complementary studies in end-stage kidney disease caregiving: a virtual reality-based mindfulness intervention to enhance psychosocial wellbeing (NUHS Seed Grant) and BOTANIC, an AI-driven chatbot for early detection of caregiver burden (NUS Academic Health Programme Fund).

Funders: National University Health System and National University of Singapore
Methods & Analysis Rehabilitation · Ongoing

Rehabilitation technology evaluation portfolio

Trial design, evaluation, and evidence synthesis for rehabilitation technologies including telerehabilitation robotics (TRUST), spinal cord stimulation and neuromodulation, robotic exoskeletons, and markerless gait analysis, spanning feasibility studies, validation work, and economic evaluation.

Collaborators: Tan Tock Seng Hospital Rehabilitation Medicine and the Rehabilitation Research Institute of Singapore
Programme Support IRIS · Ongoing

Methodological backbone for a hospital-wide translational research support service, providing clinician investigators with systematic review infrastructure, health economic modelling, qualitative research capability, and AI development support across the T2 to T3 translational stages.

Setting: Clinical Research and Innovation Office, Tan Tock Seng Hospital
